# Phase 7 — Red-Team (per-sprint sweeps)

> **What this phase does:** Stress-test the chosen model on three sweeps: adversarial perturbation, out-of-distribution input, demographic skew. Find failure modes before deployment.
> **Why it exists:** A model that scores high on the holdout can still fail on adversarial inputs, OOD content, or demographic minorities. Phase 7 catches these BEFORE the deployment gate.
> **You're here because:** Phase 6 set thresholds. Phase 7 stresses the resulting auto-decision rule.
> **Key concepts you'll see:** adversarial perturbation, OOD robustness, demographic-skew calibration, severity ranking, fairness-deferred-to-Week-7

## 4. Quick reference (30 sec, generic)

### Adversarial perturbation

Small input changes designed to flip the model's decision. Image: random pixel noise, JPEG compression, FGSM-style gradient attacks. Text: typos, unicode confusables (visually identical Cyrillic letters), leetspeak, LLM paraphrase. The cheap perturbations (typos, low-quality JPEG) are the realistic adversarial threat in moderation — adversaries don't usually have gradient access, but they do have keyboards.

> **Deeper treatment:** [appendix/04-evaluation/pr-curve.md](./appendix/04-evaluation/pr-curve.md)

### OOD robustness

Performance on inputs unlike the training distribution. Image: rare aspect ratios, low-light, novel meme formats. Text: code-mixed languages, dialects, jargon. Tonight's 5-market language span (Singlish, Malay, Bahasa Indonesia, Tagalog, Thai code-mixing) is a real OOD concern — the labelled set may over-represent one market.

> **Deeper treatment:** [appendix/02-data/data-audit.md](./appendix/02-data/data-audit.md)

### Demographic-skew calibration

Brier (calibration error) per demographic slice. If Brier on Singlish posts is 0.08 but Brier on Bahasa Indonesia posts is 0.18, the model is well-calibrated for the first market and miscalibrated for the second. Calibration drift across demographic slices means the queue allocator routes posts from those markets to wrong tiers.

> **Deeper treatment:** [appendix/06-monitoring/calibration-decay.md](./appendix/06-monitoring/calibration-decay.md)

### Severity ranking

(Probability of occurrence × Blast radius in $). A finding that costs $5M/year in expected harm is high severity even if it's rare; a finding that costs $500/year is low even if it happens daily. Ranking forces budget allocation against actual risk, not against drama.

> **Deeper treatment:** [appendix/07-governance/hitl-patterns.md](./appendix/07-governance/hitl-patterns.md)

### Fairness-deferred-to-Week-7

Phase 14 (full disparate-impact analysis across protected classes) is deferred to Week 7. Phase 7 tonight covers demographic-skew calibration (a precursor) but NOT full fairness analysis. Deferring SILENTLY is BLOCKED — every Phase 7 journal must include the explicit deferral line for audit-trail. Rubric D4 zeros silent-deferred fairness.

> **Deeper treatment:** [appendix-a-lessons.md](./appendix-a-lessons.md)
