Week 6

What Is Deep Learning?

Neural networks with many layers — each layer learns increasingly abstract patterns.

Layer N: High-level concepts (faces, objects, sentiments)
Layer 3: Combinations (eyes, noses, wheels)
Layer 2: Simple shapes (curves, corners, textures)
Layer 1: Raw features (edges, colors, pixel patterns)
Input: Raw data (pixels, characters, audio samples)

Key insight: Classical ML needs you to engineer features. Deep learning learns its own features. That's the breakthrough.

Week 6

RNN/LSTM — Understanding Sequences

Some data has order: text reads left to right, stock prices move through time, video is a sequence of frames.

RNN (Recurrent Neural Network)

  • Has "memory" — output depends on previous inputs
  • Good for short sequences
  • Struggles with long-range dependencies

LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory)

  • Solves the "forgetting" problem
  • Gates control what to remember vs. forget
  • Good for time series, speech, music

Note: Transformers have largely replaced RNN/LSTM for text, but they remain useful for time series and real-time sensor data.

Week 6

Transformers — The Attention Revolution

The architecture behind ChatGPT, Claude, DALL-E, and nearly every modern AI breakthrough.

The Key Idea: Attention

Instead of reading left-to-right, the model looks at all words simultaneously and learns which words relate to which. "The bank by the river" vs. "the bank approved the loan" — same word, different meaning. Attention figures this out.

Why It Matters

  • Processes entire sequences in parallel (fast)
  • Captures long-range relationships (accurate)
  • Scales to billions of parameters (powerful)
  • Works for text, images, audio, video, code

"Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al., 2017) — the paper that launched the current AI revolution.

Week 6

Large Language Models

Transformers trained on massive text datasets. They learn language patterns so well they can generate, summarize, translate, and reason.

Pre-training

Trained on trillions of words from the internet. Learns grammar, facts, reasoning patterns. Expensive: millions of dollars.

Fine-tuning

Adapt the pre-trained model to your specific task with smaller, curated data. Cheaper, faster, domain-specific.

Prompting

No training at all — just tell the model what to do. Zero-shot or few-shot. Cheapest, most flexible, but less reliable.

Your decision: Fine-tune when you need consistent, domain-specific behavior. Prompt when you need flexibility and speed.

Fine-tuning vs. Prompting

Factor Fine-tuning Prompting
Cost $1K–$100K+ upfront Pay per API call
Time to deploy Days to weeks Minutes
Consistency High — learned behavior Variable — depends on prompt
Data needed Hundreds to thousands of examples Zero to a few examples
Flexibility Fixed to trained task Can adapt on the fly
Best for Repeated, high-volume tasks Exploration, prototyping, varied tasks
Week 6

Multi-Modal AI

The real world isn't single-modality. A meme is text + image. A TikTok is video + audio + text. Multi-modal AI understands all of them together.

What It Does

  • Image + text: "What's in this photo?"
  • Video + audio: content moderation at scale
  • Text + image generation: DALL-E, Midjourney
  • Document understanding: charts, tables, forms

Why It Matters for Media

  • A harmless image + harmful text = harmful meme
  • Audio tone changes meaning of words
  • Context requires understanding multiple signals
  • Single-modality AI misses these combinations

Week 6

Decision: What Counts as "Harmful"?

This is a policy decision, not a technical one. The model enforces rules — you write the rules.

Clear Cases

  • Child exploitation (universal)
  • Terrorism / incitement to violence
  • Personally identifiable information exposure
  • Copyright infringement

Gray Areas

  • Satire vs. hate speech
  • News reporting vs. glorifying violence
  • Art with nudity vs. explicit content
  • Political speech vs. misinformation

The hard truth: AI can classify with high accuracy on clear cases. Gray areas still need human judgment. Your product must decide where the line is.

Week 6

Decision: Modality Integration

A meme with a cute puppy image and the text "I want to destroy all humans" is harmful. Either modality alone misses it.

Single Modality (Simpler)

  • Separate text classifier + image classifier
  • Combine scores with rules
  • Misses cross-modal meaning
  • Cheaper to build and maintain

Multi-Modal (More Accurate)

  • Joint model that sees text + image together
  • Understands cross-modal relationships
  • Catches memes, sarcasm, context
  • More expensive, needs multi-modal training data
Week 6

Decision: Automation Boundary

How much should AI decide on its own vs. flag for humans?

Auto-Approve: High confidence clean content (>99% sure it's safe)
Human Review Queue: Uncertain cases (60–99% confidence)
Auto-Remove: High confidence violation (>99% sure it's harmful)

The trade-off: Auto-remove more = faster, cheaper, but more false positives (legitimate content removed). Auto-remove less = fairer, but harmful content stays up longer and human costs increase.

Week 6

Decision: Cultural Context

Content acceptability varies dramatically across cultures and jurisdictions.

Examples

  • Satire of political leaders: protected in some countries, illegal in others
  • Religious imagery: art in one context, blasphemy in another
  • Gestures: thumbs up is positive in the West, offensive elsewhere

Product Implications

  • One global policy or region-specific rules?
  • Who defines "acceptable" for each market?
  • How do you train AI on cultural nuance?
  • Local reviewers for local content?

Reality: Most platforms use a global baseline (child safety, terrorism) + regional overlays. The AI detects; local teams interpret.

Week 6

Modern AI: Vision-Language Models

CLIP

OpenAI's model that understands images and text together. "Describe this image" or "Find images matching this text." Zero-shot visual classification.

Vision-Language Models

GPT-4V, Claude Vision, Gemini. Can reason about images: read charts, identify objects, understand scenes in context.

AI Safety

As models get more capable, safety becomes critical. Alignment, red-teaming, content filters, responsible deployment practices.

The trend: Multi-modal models are converging. Soon, one model will handle text, image, video, and audio natively. The question shifts from "can AI do this?" to "should it?"
