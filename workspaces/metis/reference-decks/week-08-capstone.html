GANs — Generative Adversarial Networks

Two neural networks competing against each other to generate increasingly realistic outputs.

Generator

The Forger

  • Creates fake data (images, text, audio)
  • Tries to fool the discriminator
  • Gets better at generating realistic outputs

Discriminator

The Detective

  • Tries to distinguish real from fake
  • Gets better at detecting fakes
  • Pushes the generator to improve

Applications: Synthetic training data (generate 10,000 defect images from 100), deepfakes, drug discovery, art generation, data augmentation.

Graph Neural Networks

Modeling Relationships

Not all data is tables or images. Some data is about connections: social networks, supply chains, molecular structures.

How They Work

  • Data represented as nodes (entities) and edges (relationships)
  • Each node learns from its neighbors
  • Information propagates through the graph
  • Captures structural patterns traditional ML misses

Applications

  • Fraud detection: suspicious transaction networks
  • Drug discovery: molecular property prediction
  • Supply chain: disruption propagation modeling
  • Social: influence spread, community detection
Week 8

Multi-Agent Orchestration

One agent is useful. Multiple agents coordinating is transformative.

How It Works

  • Orchestrator: assigns tasks, manages flow
  • Specialist agents: each handles a domain
  • Communication: agents share context
  • Conflict resolution: when agents disagree

Example: Smart Factory

  • QC Agent: inspects products
  • Maintenance Agent: schedules repairs
  • Production Agent: optimizes throughput
  • Safety Agent: monitors workplace
  • Orchestrator: balances competing goals

The challenge: Multi-agent systems can have emergent behaviors. Testing and safety become exponentially harder.

Week 8

ISO 27001 — Information Security

The international standard for information security management. If you build AI products that handle data, you need to know this.

What It Covers

  • Risk assessment and treatment
  • Access control and authentication
  • Data encryption and protection
  • Incident management and response
  • Business continuity planning

Why It Matters for AI

  • AI models are intellectual property
  • Training data often contains PII
  • Model outputs can leak sensitive info
  • Enterprise clients require certification
  • Regulatory compliance (PDPA, GDPR)
Week 8

ISO 42001 — AI Management System

The world's first international standard specifically for AI. Published 2023. Sets the framework for responsible AI development and deployment.

Key Requirements

  • AI risk assessment framework
  • Transparency and explainability standards
  • Bias and fairness evaluation
  • Human oversight mechanisms
  • Continuous monitoring and improvement

For Your Products

  • Document how your AI makes decisions
  • Test for bias across demographics
  • Define human override procedures
  • Monitor model performance over time
  • Maintain audit trails

Competitive advantage: ISO42001 certification signals trust. Early adopters will win enterprise contracts.

What Makes a Great Pitch

From a VC perspective — what investors actually look for:

Do This

  • Start with the problem — make the pain real
  • Show, don't tell — live demo beats slides
  • Know your numbers — TAM, pricing, unit economics
  • Be honest about risks — awareness builds trust
  • End with a clear ask — what do you need?

Don't Do This

  • Lead with the technology (nobody cares about your stack)
  • Claim "no competition" (always a red flag)
  • Use jargon without explaining it
  • Bury the demo at the end
  • Run over time (instant disqualification in real pitches)
